What is Discrete Probability?

Discrete probability is the study of events with a countable number of possible outcomes. It provides a mathematical framework to measure uncertainty and predict the likelihood of different outcomes in situations involving randomness. By assigning probabilities to each outcome in a defined sample space, we can analyze and make informed decisions about uncertain events. This area forms the basis for understanding chance, risk, and variability in various real-world and theoretical contexts.

🎲 Real-Life Example

Imagine you're applying for a scholarship and there are 100 applicants but only 10 will be chosen. The probability of being selected is 10/100 = 0.1 or 10%.
Understanding this helps you plan better and manage expectations realistically.

Understanding this basic probability doesn’t just set realistic expectations—it guides you toward smarter strategies and better-prepared solutions.
