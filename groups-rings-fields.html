What are Groups, Rings, and Fields?

These are algebraic structures with specific rules.
A group is a set with one operation that behaves nicely - a non-empty set with a binary operation defined on it such that the operation is associative, an identity element exists, and every element has an inverse.
A ring adds a second operation (like addition and multiplication), and a field is a ring where every non-zero element has a multiplicative inverse. They help define how elements interact and are key in coding, logic, and computation.

Reflection

At first, I struggled to tell the difference between groups, rings, and fields. But by exploring their properties and seeing them in logic or number systems, I realized how they build on one another. Understanding these helped me appreciate the deeper structure behind even simple arithmetic.
Rings are basically just groups with another operation added to it, and fields also just have an additional characteristics.
