What is Graph Theory?

Graph theory studies structures made up of nodes (also called vertices) and connections between them (called edges). It models relationships and pathways, whether in maps, social networks, or data structures.

Reflection

I used to think graphs were just bar charts or line plots. But in discrete math, I learned they’re actually about connections — like routes, friendships, or links in a web. I now see how powerful and flexible graphs are in representing real-world systems.

🌐 Real-Life Example

Think of Google Maps. It uses graphs to find the shortest path from one place to another. Each location is a node, and roads are edges. Algorithms like Dijkstra’s help calculate optimal routes.

📌 More Applications

  • 📶 Internet Routing: The internet is made of interconnected nodes, like routers and servers.
  • 👥 Social Networks: Platforms like Facebook or LinkedIn are graph-based — showing people and their connections.
  • 🧠 Neural Networks: AI systems are often modeled as graphs where nodes are neurons.
  • 🛒 Recommendation Engines: Graphs track how users relate to products to suggest what to buy next.
